AI Initiative Boosts Student Performance in Tonk's Government Schools

The 'Padhai with AI' initiative in Rajasthan's Tonk district uses artificial intelligence to improve students' performance, particularly in mathematics.

Jun 22, 2025Source: Visive.ai
Featured Image Placeholder

AI Initiative Enhances Student Performance in Tonk

An AI-based initiative, 'Padhai with AI', has significantly improved the academic performance of students in Rajasthan's Tonk district. Launched to assist students with challenging subjects, particularly mathematics, the initiative has seen remarkable success in its first academic session, with 95% of secondary students passing with flying colours.

Addressing Challenges in Mathematics

The program was introduced by Tonk Collector Saumya Jha after identifying fear and anxiety about mathematics as a significant barrier to student success. The initiative uses a dedicated web portal that combines technology with education, offering remedial practices, drills, and personalized learning.

Detailed Action Plan

A detailed action plan was prepared for a three-month academic session, targeting 10th class students and setting clear goals for 2025. A total of 351 government schools were selected for the initiative. The web portal provides a comprehensive learning experience, helping students solve textbook problems and practice new questions of a similar pattern.

Impressive Results

After the results were declared in May, it was evident that the percentage of students who passed in Tonk was higher than the state average. There was also a 5% increase in the number of students passing in the first division. Ms. Jha noted, “An impressive jump was witnessed in the performance in mathematics, which was earlier a dreaded subject for children.”

Future Prospects

The success of 'Padhai with AI' in Tonk highlights the potential of AI in education. By leveraging technology, the initiative has not only improved academic performance but also reduced the fear and anxiety associated with challenging subjects. This success story could serve as a model for other districts and states looking to enhance their educational systems through innovative solutions.
