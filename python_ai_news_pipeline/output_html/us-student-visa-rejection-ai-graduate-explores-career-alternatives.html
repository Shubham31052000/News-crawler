US Student Visa Rejection: AI Graduate Explores Career Alternatives

A young Indian AI and Machine Learning graduate faces a pivotal decision after his US student visa rejection, sparking a broader debate on the value of international education.

Jun 22, 2025Source: Visive.ai

A young Indian tech graduate is at a crossroads after being denied an F-1 visa for the United States. With aspirations to pursue a Master’s in Data Science, the 22-year-old is now reassessing his future, a scenario that resonates with many in the country’s extensive tech talent pool.

Navigating Post-Rejection Choices

Armed with a Bachelor’s degree in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, the student had set his sights on advancing his academic journey in the US. However, the rejection of his student visa has prompted a deeper reflection on the value of overseas education in a competitive global market.

Seeking Advice on Reddit

Taking to Reddit for advice, the student sought input on whether to reapply for the US visa, explore options in Europe, or start working in India. He expressed concerns about the modest entry-level salaries in the domestic tech industry, hoping a master’s abroad would provide better opportunities.

Diverse Perspectives on International Education

His post garnered a range of responses, reflecting a broader shift in perceptions of international education. One user suggested, “Moving to another country without much experience won’t get you a job with higher pay. You’re better off getting a job in India than doing a master’s.”

Another commenter pointed out the oversaturation of the data science and development fields in Europe, particularly the UK, where many roles are outsourced to countries like India. “Try doing your master’s in India,” the user advised.

Weighing the Cost-Benefit

The exchange highlights growing concerns among students that an expensive international degree may no longer guarantee a high-paying job or smoother immigration pathway. As countries tighten visa policies and outsource more tech work, Indian graduates are increasingly evaluating the cost-benefit of studying abroad.

Future Considerations

With six months before the next application cycle, the student remains undecided but open to feedback. “I won’t say my thoughts are perfect. If I’m making any mistakes or overlooking something, please feel free to point it out,” he wrote.

Conclusion

The student’s dilemma is a microcosm of the broader challenges faced by young tech professionals in India. As they navigate the complex landscape of international education and career opportunities, the value of a well-considered plan and open-mindedness cannot be overstated.

Frequently Asked Questions

What options do Indian tech graduates have after a US student visa rejection?

They can reapply for the US visa, explore options in Europe, or start working in India. Each path has its pros and cons, and the choice depends on personal goals and circumstances.

Why are some users advising against pursuing a master’s degree abroad?

Some users believe that the cost and effort of an international degree may not guarantee better job opportunities, especially given the oversaturation of tech roles in many countries.

What are the main concerns for Indian students considering international education?

The main concerns include the high cost of education, the uncertainty of job prospects, and the tightening of visa policies in many countries.

How can Indian tech graduates benefit from staying and working in India?

Indian tech graduates can gain valuable experience, build a professional network, and contribute to the growing tech industry in India, which offers numerous opportunities for growth.

What advice do experts give to students facing visa rejections?

Experts advise students to reassess their goals, explore alternative paths, and remain open to feedback and new opportunities. Networking and continuous learning are also crucial.
