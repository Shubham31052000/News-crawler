OpenAI Ditches 'io' Brand for Jony Ive Hardware Project

OpenAI has removed all references to the 'io' brand, its planned AI hardware startup with Jony Ive, due to a trademark dispute.

Jun 22, 2025Source: Visive.ai

OpenAI has scrubbed all mentions of the “io” brand from its website and social media channels. This planned AI hardware startup, which was to be led by Jony Ive, faced a significant setback due to a trademark dispute. The decision to remove the 'io' branding reflects the company's commitment to avoiding legal complications and maintaining a clear focus on its core mission.

OpenAI's Strategic Move

OpenAI's move to remove the 'io' brand is a strategic decision aimed at mitigating legal risks. The company is known for its pioneering work in artificial intelligence and has always been cautious about its branding and intellectual property. The 'io' brand, which was intended to represent a new line of AI hardware, has now been completely phased out from all public communications.

Background on the 'io' Project

The 'io' project was initially announced with great fanfare, promising to bring cutting-edge AI hardware to market. Jony Ive, the former chief design officer at Apple, was a key figure in the project, leveraging his expertise in design and innovation. However, the trademark dispute has forced OpenAI to reassess its branding and marketing strategies.

Impact on the AI Hardware Market

The removal of the 'io' brand could have significant implications for the AI hardware market. Competitors and industry observers are closely watching how OpenAI will navigate this challenge. The company is expected to continue its efforts in developing advanced AI hardware, but under a different brand name.

Legal Implications

Trademark disputes are common in the tech industry, and they can have far-reaching consequences. OpenAI's decision to remove the 'io' brand is a proactive step to avoid potential legal battles. The company is likely to focus on rebranding the project and ensuring that all future products and services are clearly differentiated from existing trademarks.

Future Plans and Vision

Despite the setback, OpenAI remains committed to its vision of advancing AI technology. The company has a strong track record of innovation and is expected to continue pushing the boundaries of what is possible with AI hardware. Jony Ive's involvement in the project is still a significant asset, and his design expertise will likely play a crucial role in the development of future products.

Conclusion

The removal of the 'io' brand is a temporary setback for OpenAI, but it is a necessary step to ensure the company's long-term success. As the AI hardware market continues to evolve, OpenAI's focus on innovation and strategic decision-making will be key to its continued growth and leadership in the industry.
